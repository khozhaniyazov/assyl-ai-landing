# Project Research Summary

**Project:** Assyl-ai landing page  
**Domain:** Pediatric speech therapy lead-generation and intake operations (Aktau, Kazakhstan)  
**Researched:** 2026-04-05  
**Confidence:** **MEDIUM**

## Executive Summary

Assyl-ai is not a generic content site; it is a WhatsApp-first demand capture and intake system for a high-trust, high-sensitivity service category (children’s speech and development). Across the research, the winning pattern is consistent: parents choose quickly when they see clear expected progress, trust signals, and low-friction next steps. The product should therefore prioritize outcome clarity, fast qualification, and local credibility rather than feature complexity.

The strongest recommended approach is to build around a **structured conversion + intake operating model**: segment-aware messaging on-page, strong objection handling, channel-coded WhatsApp entry points, and CRM-backed lead lifecycle tracking. This keeps growth and operations connected, so marketing claims, first-response SLAs, and booked/paid outcomes can be measured as one funnel.

The key risk cluster is also consistent across files: overpromising outcomes, weak evidence depth, and compliance failures in consent/data handling (especially child-related context in chat). Mitigation is clear: explicit consent capture, minimum-data intake, bounded WhatsApp usage for triage/scheduling only, and disciplined proof standards (time-bound, conditional, documented claims).

## Key Findings

### Positioning (POSITIONING.md)

- Market is crowded but fragmented; many competitors are vague about timelines and outcomes.
- Best differentiation for Assyl-ai: **early measurable progress (2–5 window) + transparent process + fast WhatsApp response**.
- Keep “qualification-before-price” flow, but reduce hidden-pricing anxiety with contextual ranges after qualification/FAQ.
- Highest-leverage trust stack: quantified outcomes with scope/method, specialist credentials, process transparency, response-time promise, and local legitimacy.

### Audience (AUDIENCE.md)

- Core ICP is mothers of children 2–5 with early concern signals; secondary segments include school-readiness parents, evidence-seeking fathers, and previously disappointed families.
- Decision order is explicit: outcome clarity → trust/safety → diagnostic clarity → logistics speed → value framing → social proof.
- High-frequency objections are known and mappable to product/content requirements (e.g., 30/60/90 progress checkpoints, first-3-sessions explanation, father-forward summary).
- Messaging that works in this context: supportive urgency, measurable but realistic outcomes, plain-language plan, empathetic tone, local proof.

### Channels & Measurement (CHANNELS.md)

- Priority execution order for next 90 days: **Maps + Referrals first**, then Instagram, then Search intent capture.
- WhatsApp-first funnel has attribution blind spots; solve with UTM discipline + prefilled WA source codes + lightweight CRM schema.
- Realistic baseline funnel ranges are provided and usable for planning targets.
- Success criteria should focus on data completeness and channel-level decision readiness (not vanity metrics).

### Compliance & Intake Ops (COMPLIANCE-OPS.md)

- Treat all inbound leads as personal data with potential health sensitivity; adopt minimum-data intake and explicit consent by default.
- Mandatory controls: required consent checkbox + versioned consent logging, intake script guardrails, SLA tracker, opt-out stop rules.
- WhatsApp should remain for triage/scheduling/reminders—not deep clinical history storage.
- Data lifecycle controls (30–90 day cleanup for dormant leads) and business-account-only handling are critical to reduce legal/operational risk.

## Top Opportunities

1. **Own the “measurable early progress” category position in Aktau** with specific, conditional milestone framing.
2. **Convert faster than competitors via response-time excellence** (≤5 min acknowledgment, ≤15 min human qualification).
3. **Build a proof engine** (structured case library + dated metrics method notes) to outperform generic testimonial competitors.
4. **Create channel-to-revenue visibility quickly** using WA source codes and lightweight CRM fields before heavy tooling.
5. **Operationalize audience segmentation** (A1/A2/A3/A4) into prefilled WA scripts and objection-specific content blocks.

## Top Risks

1. **Overpromising outcomes** → trust/complaint exposure.  
   **Control:** range-based claims, clear conditions, “results vary” language, evidence timestamping.
2. **Consent/evidence gaps in data handling** → compliance risk.  
   **Control:** required checkbox, consent text versioning, timestamp/source logging, privacy notice.
3. **WhatsApp-only operational fragility** (spam reports, blocked users, device sprawl).  
   **Control:** strict message relevance, capped follow-ups, business-managed accounts only, opt-out SOP.
4. **Attribution ambiguity across IG/Search/Maps/Call** → bad budget decisions.  
   **Control:** UTM discipline, WA prefill codes, CRM first/last touch fields, weekly funnel report.
5. **Single-page trust deficit if proof remains shallow** → conversion ceiling.  
   **Control:** specialist credentials depth, process transparency, “first 7 days” expectations, local case specificity.

## Explicit Requirement Implications for Planning Docs

### Phase 1 — Foundation: Compliance + Measurement (first)
**Rationale:** Required to safely scale acquisition and make roadmap decisions with reliable data.  
**Must deliver:**
- Required consent checkbox (not pre-checked) + privacy notice link.
- Consent event logging (timestamp, source URL, consent text version).
- UTM and channel-specific WhatsApp prefill codes.
- CRM schema fields: lead_id, first/last touch, wa_code, qualification, booking, paid start.
- SLA dashboard and enforcement rules (acknowledgment/qualification/follow-up caps).
**Pitfalls avoided:** compliance violations, attribution blind spots, slow-response leakage.

### Phase 2 — Messaging & Objection Architecture
**Rationale:** Audience research is mature enough to implement now; directly affects conversion quality.  
**Must deliver:**
- ICP-specific copy variants (A1 vs A2 priority).
- Objection-first FAQ covering top 6 objections.
- 30/60/90-day progress checkpoint section.
- “First 3 sessions” adaptation/comfort explanation block.
- Risk-reduction sentence adjacent to every major CTA.
**Pitfalls avoided:** generic positioning, fear backlash, weak trust transfer.

### Phase 3 — Channel Systemization (90-day growth loop)
**Rationale:** Once foundation and messaging are stable, channel testing becomes decision-grade.  
**Must deliver:**
- Maps/2GIS profile optimization and review/photo cadence.
- Referral playbook (post-milestone ask + partner QR handouts).
- Instagram creative testing matrix (pain-point reels, progress stories).
- Search capture for brand/symptom keywords with FAQ snippet alignment.
- Weekly channel report and channel-level conversion/CAC readout.
**Pitfalls avoided:** premature ad spend, non-comparable channel data, wrong scaling bets.

### Phase 4 — Trust & Proof System (scale-ready differentiation)
**Rationale:** Sustainable moat comes from evidence quality, not louder claims.  
**Must deliver:**
- Structured case library (age, baseline, timeline, measurable outcomes).
- Testimonial quality standard (≥50% measurable-result format).
- Specialist credibility expansion (credentials, specialization, environment visuals).
- Claim governance policy (method notes, update cadence, disclaimer standards).
**Pitfalls avoided:** credibility decay, claims disputes, conversion stagnation at higher traffic.
